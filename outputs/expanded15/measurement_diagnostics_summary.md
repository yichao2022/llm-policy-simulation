# Measurement Diagnostics Summary

## Interpretation Guidelines

### 1. The battery is NOT psychometrically validated — say this clearly

AP (α = −0.239) and CA (α = 0.020) have very poor internal consistency. ST (α = 0.522) is weak. The battery was designed as a theory-driven exploratory instrument, not a validated scale, and the diagnostics confirm this limitation. Report these α values transparently in the paper, but do not describe them as "reliability" or "psychometric properties" — use "exploratory internal-consistency diagnostics" throughout.

### 2. H1 null is robust regardless of how you slice it

Most alternative PVC constructions (raw-score, leave-one-dimension-out) preserve the null result. Critically, **0 of 20 items**, when individually excluded, change the H1 conclusion. This means the null is not driven by any particular item or weighting choice — it reflects a genuinely weak association between the PVC composite and burden effects at the model level.

### 3. CO is both the best dimension and the most interesting exception

CO has the highest internal consistency by far (α = 0.869). But when CO is removed from PVC, the composite becomes a significant predictor of burden effects (p = 0.047, R² = 0.270). This is **not** a salvage — it's a diagnostic signal:

- The theoretical weighting on CO (−1) may not be appropriate for the vaccination access context.
- CO may be carrying directional signal that offsets signal from the other dimensions under the current weighting scheme.
- This does not rescue H1, but it suggests the PVC formula itself may need domain-specific calibration.
- Treat this as **exploratory** — a finding worth noting in robustness checks, not a result to build a new hypothesis on.

### Bottom line for the paper

> The battery is not a validated scale, but the H1 null is not a measurement artifact. The absence of a PVC-burden association is robust to alternative weighting, item exclusion, and dimension exclusion. The CO exception is analytically useful but exploratory.
